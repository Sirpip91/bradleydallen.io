# ABSTRACT
What makes the application of multimedia data processing within the Internet of Things IoT
secure from cyberattacks and vulnerabilities? In this survey we will go over some of the major
security threats and attacks that have been found with the improvement and use of IoT in
conjunction with multimedia processing. We will first start off by explaining and showing what
Internet of Things IoT consists of and how it is being developed today. Then by going over four
conference papers regarding IoT and some multimedia developments for the technology, we will
see details on cybersecurity within the IoT technology. 

Attacks such as brute force, web crawlers, and access to sensitive private information will all be covered in regards with IoT devices. After
reviewing the papers, we will evaluate each as a group and see how multimedia devices with IoT
can improve to be more secure with its operation. In conclusion with this survey, we will see the
common details of the papers and how each are leading to ensure secure multimedia data
processing within IoT devices. To conclude the entire goal of the survey we will look at how the
future of IoT devices and how security will apply to them as the technology grows.

# INTRODUCTION
The technology Internet of Things is commonly referred to as IoT. If we break down what is IoT
it is we will see that “IOT is not a single technology but a mix of technologies existing and
evolving Big Data, Cloud computing, RFID, Lora, Sigfox…” 1. The use of the “things” in
IoT can be distinguished as multimedia devices and software within computer science. The
question is that with the large development of IoT technology, will the security of IoT developed
with it. With IoT being used in many fields, operations, software, and many more applications,
the security of IoT devices is very critical to reduce cyber-attacks.


The main types of security issues are within networks, privacy, databases, devices, and public
access. Throughout this survey we will see how IoT devices are created, improved, installed, and
implemented such that we can understand the security issues that come with them. All
improvements in software and hardware will come with security vulnerabilities and the security
must improve with the development. Therefore, in this survey our center of attention will be on
the improvements of IoT devices, and the security improvements must come with them to ensure
multimedia data processing will be secure, safe, and reliable for all users.

# 2. RELATED WORK
IoT is being used in many different types of development throughout the world. This worldwide
development is contributing to its fast growth, but “Internet of Things IoT is still an
unregulated domain and there are no agreed standards which leads to fragmentation” 2. This
can lead to many different methodologies of security and cause holes for exploit in the
development. This work focuses on the lack of security in the development cycle of IoT systems.
Utilizing the holes in security, many brute force attacks are used for IoT devices. With examples
of brute force attacks with IoT devices 3, we can see that the security of IoT devices important
no matter what the field it is involved in. This work goes directly into how attackers are
managing to access nodes in devices such as tvs, refrigerators, and any type of technology that is
connected through some form of network. This causes a safety and privacy issue with
homeowners, for example those using technology products in their homes.


The improvements of multimedia data processing are even having an impact on those with
disabilities in the world. IoT is being developed to help improve comprehension and accessibility
with deaf and blind people around the world. Auditory data transformation will be used to help
explain what is going on for example on a public transportation system 4. These devices can be
installed in public areas to help the public in many ways. Although this is generally a good thing,
this leads the public to be able to tamper with and use security threats to access the devices.

# 3. OVERVIEW AND EVALUTATION
In this section we will go over the security threats with the development of IoT as well as how
security threats are being handled in devices. Securing multimedia data processing within IoT is
based on the software, hardware, and access to networks that the devices are using. Each of these
aspects will determine how secure a device is and the amount of protection to threats it will have.

# 3.1 What does mean search engine for IOT
The first paper “What does mean search engine for IOT or IOT search engine” 1 has the idea of
explaining and showcasing the power of IoT. Through showing the reader test cases and
examples of search engines being developed solely for the purpose of using IoT principles. This
paper is eye opening for many seeing that the availability of connected multimedia devices on
personal computers are accessible by many websites. For example, many websites have cookies
enables that will determine if the user has a webcam or microphone connected when visiting.
This paper showcases three search engines used for IoT data collection. Search engines used for
the data in the paper are Shodan, Censys, and Topical. Each of theses IoT search engines focus
on a different aspect of multimedia data collection. The type of data collected with these search
engines are IP address, port, location, hostname, devices connected to the network, and even
coordinates of the user.


The search engines that involve the use of IoT multimedia data collection cause many security
and privacy problems with users. First, the data collection of the users information and location
is many times hidden within the access of cookies. This makes the collection of the data kind of
sneaky. Second, this information is being stored on a database somewhere without knowing if the
data is secure enough against cyber-attacks. Knowing a homeowners camera and network
information can allow attackers to manipulate and even assist in setting ups robbery to
homeowners location. One of the development issues this paper goes over is that IoT search
engines need to “protect privacy of the proprietors of connected devices and secure their
database…” 1. Even though the information and data that is collected by the search engine may
not be harmful, it is through access and storage of the information that is the security issue. The
development of search engines for IoT devices must be developed parallel with the security of
IoT devices as well to ensure that all data and security issues will be able to be addressed.

# 3.2 Towards Designing Open and Secure IoT Systems
The second paper “Towards Designing Open and Secure IoT Systems: Insights for Practitioners”
2 inspects the developers and designers of IoT systems and devices. Many of the developers for
IoT systems are focused on creating the new technology that will help improve the world in
some way. Most of the design fundamentals is in the flexibility and customizability of the IoT
system. The IoT system must be created and developed to be able to have a functional system
and this is the main goal of the developers. Although this is a great tactic to focus on during
development, this is where the security threats and holes are created. Based on the study on
practitioners and developers it is seen that, “practitioners, designers and manufacturers of IoT
system do not consider security and privacy as a big concern from start, e.g., they try to apply
security retroactively or as an add-on” 2. I agree that the functionality of the IoT is very
important for project and company reasons, but security and privacy should not be left out of the
development process. In most cases if a system is not developed with security in mind it will
lacking in security throughout the entire system. Leading to a security and privacy system that
will be developed to fix problems instead of preventing them.


We then come to the problem that when designing an open and secure IoT system, security and
privacy is not mandatory. There are no rulebooks when developing and designing a IoT system.
This is a critical problem regarding security since no developer will have the motive to put in
extra effort to create secure software and hardware connections. The practitioners of the IoT
device and system development are the sole problem and solution to security issues involving
IoT multimedia data management. When we think of a solution to the secure management of the
data from multimedia processes, we must start from the source. Developers and the managers of
the systems have a large part to play regarding IoT systems and because the technology is new
many issues are overlooked. The paper concludes that since there are no general guidelines to
address security and privacy issues regarding IoT development it is a mindset and awareness
problem.

# 3.3 IoT Lotto: Utilizing IoT Devices in Brute-Force Attacks
The third paper “IoT Lotto: Utilizing IoT Devices in Brute-Force Attacks” 3 focuses on
explaining how brute force attacks with IoT devices are caused because of lack of security
implementation. Brute force attacks are explained as trying all possible ways to break into a
device or system until the device is broke into. This form of attack is very common in the world
of cybersecurity and passwords. This attack can be very detrimental to a user of a IoT device for
example someone who uses a Bluetooth battery powered door opener for their home. An
example attack would be to brute force the encryption and decryption of the door opener until the
passcode is found. The paper goes over an example of an IoT attack that “took place in 2014
when malicious attackers broke into over 100,000 home IoT devices like televisions and fridges”
3. As the amount of IoT device grows the number of attacks will grow with them. If people are
using devices that have access to the internet, then these devices should have the upmost amount
of security and privacy development. Even though a refrigerator may seem like it cannot cause a
problem, it can become a problem if the technology malfunctions. For example, if the fridge
temperature meter is turned off and it freezes and break causing a fire.
Some of the software components of IoT devices uses encryption and decryption technology to
implement security features. Many of the encryption and decryption ciphers that are used in
technology can and will be brute forced into using attack software. Many of the IoT devices are
issued a key that is assigned to it, allowing a key search space. Thus “if u = 4, then there are 24
possible combinations. Assuming that we have 4 IoT devices, n = 4, each device is expected to try

This will allow the brute force attack to be optimized further allowing a smaller search space for keys. The paper concludes with explaining that deploying more IoT
devices will increase the amount of sub-space keys, meaning that it would be harder to
successfully brute force attack. On the other hand, the smaller number of devices deployed the
smaller key search space would be. Meaning that as the amount of IoT multimedia devices grow
the more search space one will need for keys.

# 3.4 Enhancing Public Transit Accessibility for the Visually Impaired Using IoT
The fourth and final paper “Enhancing Public Transit Accessibility for the Visually Impaired
Using IoT and Open Data Infrastructures” 4 talks about the implementation of IoT multimedia
devices to help assist public transportation passengers that have a disability. First, the device in
the transportation system would provide real time transit information to users who need auditory
explanation. This information would be transmitted to the users phone with the use of GPS,
Cellular network, Wi-Fi, and Bluetooth. The user would be able to interact with the system
through their phone using gestures. This is a great improvement that can be made with the use of
IoT devices. Although it is a great implementation that can happen to public transportation, there
are some security issues that may come with the system. Such as the “GPS-based search allows
users to search for the timetable of trains/buses within 500m of their current position” 4. We
can see that a lot of information will be flowing from one IoT device to another such as the
location of the blind user, the train arrival time, and the times of the next trains. This information
may be used for a good cause but leads to many holes of security open for cyber-attacks. The
information that is being transferred by Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and cellular network must be
encrypted as the information may be altered by an attacker while being received. Thus, causing a
lot of security threats.


This paper is a good example of how the design and implementation of a
“TalkingTransitSYSTEM” 4 is focused on making the system work rather than designing and
implementing the security and privacy precautions at the same time of development. The paper
goes on to explain how the user can use gestures with their mobile devices to interact with the
IoT system to send and receive information. There would be IoT multimedia devices
implemented throughout the train system and so the users will be able to access the system in all
areas of the train station. This amount of IoT multimedia devices being used will increase the
risk of attacks and false information being sent to users if security and privacy is not developed
with the system. The paper goes on to conclude that they are working on improving their user
experience and “planning real-world deployment of TalkingTransit in the future” 4. With the
deployment of the IoT there are many security features that can help improve the system overall.

# 4. EVALUATION
All of the works reviewed in this survey explain how the use of IoT multimedia devices is
growing and that security development with the devices is very important. Whether the devices
be used for a homeowners fridge, a search engine, train station accessibility they are still subject
to attacks. The papers above showcase how even though the implementation of the devices mean
well, security and privacy features must be developed with them. This focus on security and
privacy regarding IoT devices is lacking, and the focus must be changed in the future. We see
that “security cannot be introduced after the product has been shipped but needs to be integrated
in all phases of the IoT system development lifecycle” 2, because fixing the issue is worse than
preventing the issue. Not only will users be very happy with the security and privacy
improvements of the devices they use in their daily lives, but the developers will have a guide on
how to go about developing the security features. Once the improvement regarding security is
implemented and developed with IoT multimedia devices, then they will start to be accepted and
developed in a manner that prevents problems instead of fixing them.


Each paper has its own idea on how the IoT devices are secure, used, and developed. For
example, one focuses on the brute force attacks that are used, one on the development of the
devices for disabled, and they all have one thing in common, the use of IoT multimedia devices
in the world. Much of the storing and transfer of data between the devices is where the problem
is. Instead of focusing on the development of the devices, the focus should be on making sure the
data and transmission is secure from alteration leading to ill data.

# CONCLUSION
This survey has gone over how the use of Internet of Things IoT devices and software are used
in the world and the security and privacy problems they have brought. By improving the security
features of the devices, we will improve the development and use of them as well. Regardless of
how the IoT devices are improved over the years, it will be useless if they are not secure.
Attackers can bring problems to any technology and making sure that the attackers are not able
to misuse the devices and data is very important. In the first paper we see that search engines
regarding IoT take the device data and store the information leading to a massive amount of
device data. In the second paper we see that the problem when developing secure IoT devices
lies within the mindset and awareness of its developers. In the third paper we see that attacking a
IoT device can be as simple as a brute force attack and there are serious security threats. The
final paper goes over how IoT is being used in public transportation to help improve the
experience of disabled people. Along with explaining the uses of IoT multimedia devices and
system these four papers show that even if the system works it does not mean it is secure.
Therefore, we can see that if IoT multimedia technology keeps developing, its security and
privacy must develop parallel with it to ensure safety for its users and developers.


# FUTURE WORK
In the future Internet of Things IoT will grow and be implemented all around the world.
Whether it be in public transportation, search engines, home devices, and even music players. We
can see that IoT multimedia devices will record data and store data for just about any use. With
IoT being more popular and used the improvement of guidelines for development are bound to
happen. This is because as the devices and use cases grow, so will the number of developers. Just
about all the uses of IoT devices and software come down to either improving a users life or
taking data from the users. Security is very important as the user is the common denominator for
both being the data and using the data. In all ensuring secure multimedia data processing within
IoT devices is extremely important as these devices can contain very sensitive data not only
about users, but the world in general.
